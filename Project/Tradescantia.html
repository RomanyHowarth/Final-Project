Tradescantia

| Spiderwort

The Tradescantia (also known as Spiderworts) are beautiful, resilient, and convenient plants. They are trailing plants that can grow very fast and can be quickly revived after being pushed to the brink of desolation. There are many varieties of Tradescantia and these instructions will be for Tradescantia Zebrina plant!

Top Tips:

  • Try not to move your plant around too much. They tend to adapt quickly to a spot but moving them to different areas frequently can stump growth while they adapt.
  • Water frequently when the top inch of soil is dry
  • These plants are incredibly easy to propagate! Once it starts to get long simply chop off the top and either plant it right back in the soil or put in water until roots grow. You'll have a whole new plant!

Sunlight

Tradescantia Zebrina do best in bright, indirect light. Place near a window that has bright light but be careful to avoid direct sun rays.

Too much light:

  • Browning tips of leaves indicating sun burn
  • Drying soil and constantly needing water indicating the sun is drying up the water.

Not enough light:

  • Discolouration - the colour or pattern on the leaves fading.
Water

These plants like for the soil to be consistently moist. Water when the top inch of soil is dry and be sure to water the soil underneath the leaves!

Too much water:

  • Drooping, yellow leaves.
  • Root rot - check the roots are not soggy, a pot with drainage holes is best!
  • Stems or leaves rotting

Not enough water:

  • Crispy, curling leaves
  • Grey, washed out appearance
  • Lack of new growth (these plants grow fast so you'll notice if it's not grown in a while)
Humidity

Your bathroom or kitchen would be a great choice for your Tradescantia Zebrina because it does best in a slightly more humid environment. They are very adaptable though so you can also mist your plant to help with humidity levels!

Humidity too high:

  • Decay and rotting of roots from excess moisture

Humidity too low:

  • Browning leaves
