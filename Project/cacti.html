Cactus

| κάκτος

The word 'cactus' comes from Latin through Ancient Greek with the plural being either 'cacti' or 'cactus'. A member of the Cactaceae plant family, a cactus is a prickly plant accustomed to desert conditions. They are easy to care for and extremely hardy, so are the perfect beginner plant for those without plant care skills!

Top Tips:

  • Handle with care! Most houseplant cacti have spikes so be sure to handle them carefully and keep them away from children and pets!
  • There is a myth that they do not need water, they do!
  • They can grow fast if you make sure to repot them in bigger pots as they grow

Sunlight

Cacti love the sun so a bright, sunny spot is perfect for them! Leaving them in the shade can result in discolouration and limited growth. Keeping them on a sunny south-facing windowsill is a good spot but too much strong sun can result in burning.

Too much light:

Most cacti can handle direct sun but too much can be harmful for some, especially those that are not used to it. For example, if you were to move a cactus into the sun after it has gotten accustomed to the shade it may burn!

These can be symptoms of too much light:

  • Browned or calloused flesh with a rougher texture than the rest of the plant

Not enough light:

While some species can tolerate lower light levels, no cactus tends to thrive in the dark with these being some symptoms of light deprivation:

  • Discolouration - the deep green can turn to a pale green
  • Unusual growth habits - Cacti will stretch to reach the light and have new strange growths which are much smaller than the rest of the plant like a skinny branch.
  • Root rot - lack of sunlight can result in the soil staying moist for too long and rotting the roots.
Water

There is a misconception that cacti do not need to be watered, but in fact they definitely do and thrive when watered correctly!

Cacti thrive best when they are watered thoroughly, and then left until the soil/ potting media is completely dried out before watering again. However, this is usually not often. Excluding winter months, cacti usually only need to be watered every 3-4 weeks. During the winter they do not need to be watered at all!

Too much water:

  • Mushy feeling
  • Browning or blackening of flesh, stem, or at the base of the plant
  • Rotting - brown or black roots suggest root rot from too much water.

Not enough water:

  • Puckering or shriveling of flesh
  • Discolouration- turning brown, dry, and/or calloused
Humidity

The relative humidity for cacti plant is between 40-60%. Anything below 40% is too dry for most cacti, although some desert species may be okay. You can use a hygrometer to measure the humidity in your house. You can also mist your cacti if it struggling with low humidity.

The higher the temperature and smaller amount of vapour in the air, the lower the humidity and vice versa.

Humidity too high:

  • Mold on the plant's stem
  • Rotting of stem

Humidity too low:

  • Discolouration- cactus turning yellow
  • Withering or death of any flowers
